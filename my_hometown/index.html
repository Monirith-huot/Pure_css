About Pnhom Penh

Phnom Penh, Cambodia’s busy capital, sits at the junction of the Mekong and Tonlé Sap rivers. It was a hub for both the Khmer Empire and French colonialists. On its walkable riverfront, lined with parks, restaurants and bars, are the ornate Royal Palace, Silver Pagoda and the National Museum, displaying artifacts from around the country. At the city’s heart is the massive, art deco Central Market

Foods

Samlor-Kako

samlor korko is a traditional Cambodian national dish

Steamed Curried Fish (Amok Trey)

Amok trey is Cambodia's national dish, a fragrant and spicy coconut fish curry

Num banh chok

Iconic tower commemorating independenceNum banh chok or Khmer noodles is a Cambodian dish that is typically served for breakfast or consumed as an afternoon snack.

Samlor machu trey

Samlar machu, meaning sour soup in Khmer, is a traditional Cambodian dish made with meat, fish, or seafood and vegetables cooked in a sour broth base.

Sankhya lapov

Sankhya lapov is a traditional Cambodian dessert that consists of a small pumpkin filled with creamy custard containing coconut milk or cream, palm sugar paste, eggs, and salt.

Lok lak

Lok lak is a Cambodian stir-fry utilizing beef, chicken, or shrimp as the key ingredients, although beef is typically the most popular option.
